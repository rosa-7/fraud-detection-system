Awareness

How scams usually work

Recognize pressure tactics, fake urgency, impersonation, and privacy harvesting before damage happens.

1. Build trust

Attackers imitate banks, delivery firms, tech support, and even colleagues.

2. Create pressure

Urgent language, countdowns, and threats are used to stop careful thinking.

3. Extract value

They request OTPs, passwords, card details, QR approvals, or remote access.

4. Escape fast

Links, numbers, and accounts can change quickly, so quick reporting matters.
